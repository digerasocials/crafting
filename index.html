THE CRAFT

Formed by Nature,
Refined by Hand.

Based on the historic coast of Wilmington, North Carolina, Cape Fear Carving brings raw, salvaged American hardwoods to life. Each block of walnut, cherry, or oak is analyzed for weeks before a single tool touches the surface.

By working in harmony with the natural defects, voids, and wood grain, the resulting sculpture is a collaboration between centuries-old organic growth and contemporary design.

THE METHODOLOGY

The Path to Mastery

01

Select Wood

Sourcing sustainable, air-dried local hardwoods like curly maple, black walnut, and white oak. The wood's moisture, grain orientation, and natural flaws dictate the sculpture outline.

02

Design Concept

Iterative sketching and physical modeling. The design captures the dynamic balance between negative space, light shadows, and structural integrity before carving begins.

03

Hand Carving

Using premium Swiss-made gouges, mallets, and fine rotary tools. Rough material is slowly removed over hundreds of hours, carving deep contours and emphasizing the wood's growth rings.

04

Finish & Polish

Meticulous hand-sanding from 120 up to 2000 grit, followed by multiple coats of museum-grade oils and natural beeswax to protect the wood and make the grain lines shimmer.
